This discovery occurred during an expedition that sought to establish trade routes with the Indies. Cabral took possession of the lands in the name of Portugal, naming them the "Island of the True Cross" ("Ilha de Vera Cruz"). From that moment, Portugal began the colonization of Brazil, exploring its natural resources, establishing villages, and introducing sugarcane agriculture. The country remained a Portuguese colony until 1822 when it achieved its independence.

One name that catches attention is "Red like an ember." For those who don't know, it is the name of Brazil. It refers to the red color of a wood used for dyeing fabrics that the Portuguese found in the country - the Brazilwood. Its origin is the Celtic word "barkino," which in Spanish became "barcino," and later Brazil.

Facts about Brazil

In addition to Portuguese, which is the official language, there are around 180 indigenous languages spoken by different native communities.

Size

Brazil is considered the fifth largest country in the world in terms of territorial area, with approximately 8.5 million square kilometers. Compared to Europe, Brazil is considerably larger. The total area of Europe is approximately 10.18 million square kilometers, which means that Brazil is about 83% the size of Europe.

Population

According to recent estimates, the population of Brazil is approximately 214 million people. That is nearly 6 times the population of Canada.

Speakers

It is estimated that the total number of Portuguese speakers, including those who have the language as a second language, is around 280 million to 300 million people around the world. It is the sixth most spoken language in terms of the number of native speakers.
